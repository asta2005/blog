Throughout the Greek era of the series, Kratos is portrayed as a Spartan warrior who becomes known as the "Ghost of Sparta" after accidentally killing his family due to the trickery of his former mentor Ares, the God of War. He later avenges the deaths of his family and becomes a god himself as the new "God of War" on killing Ares, learning himself to be the son of Zeus. Kratos embarks on several adventures in attempts to avert disaster and change his fate, portrayed as oblivious to all else and stoic, bloodthirsty, and arrogant in nature, engaging in morally ambiguous activities and acts of extreme violence, while frequently questioning himself, undergoing bouts of severe guilt and self-hate, attempting suicide several times, and generally being portrayed as a tragic figure, as installments focus on Kratos' origins and his relationships with his family and the Olympian gods. In the Norse era of the series, Kratos finds himself controlling his rage and learning how to truly be a father and mentor to his son, Atreus, helping him come to terms with his divinity.
